Tome of Strahd

Read aloud:

This card tells of history. Knowledge of the ancient will help you better understand your enemy.

First reading: six of coins - the beggar

A wounded elf has what you seek. He will part with the treasure to see his dark dreams fulfilled.

The treasure is hidden in Kasimir's hovel (chapter 5, area N9a)

Holy Symbol of Ravenkind

Read aloud:

This card tells of a powerful force for good and protection, a holy symbol of great hope.

First reading: four of coins - the merchant

Seek a cask that once contained the finest wine, of which not a drop remains.

The treasure lies in Castle Ravenloft's wine cellar (chapter 4, area K63)

Sunsword

Read aloud:

This is a card of power and strength. It tells of a weapon of vengeance: a sword of sunlight.

Search for the crypt of a wizard ordinaire. His staff if the key.

The treasure is hidden in the crypt of Gralmore Nimblenobs (chapter 4, area K84, crypt 37)

Story

Adventure Hook

Relevant 5e.tools page: https://5e.tools/adventure.html#CoS,1,adventure%20hooks

Before making characters, ask if everyone would prefer to start at level 1 or level 3.

  • Ask preference to start at level 1 or level 3
  • Level 1:
    • Use #Plea For Help party hook
    • Run #Death House to advance the characters to level 3
  • Level 3:
    • Use #Mysterious Visitors

Or, if they come up with a good way that their characters would know each other or be travelling with each other, use the "creeping fog" adventure hook.

Plea For Help

Characters are approached by #Arrigal, who has been sent to deliver a message to the party to plead for help. He is secretly working for Strahd!

If the players decide not to listen to Arrigal, they should be surrounded by the creeping fog when the leave the tavern for their next destination.
